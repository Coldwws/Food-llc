Our Menu

Chicken

Broiler Chicken: Raised specifically for meat production, broiler chickens grow quickly and are typically harvested at about 6 to 7 weeks of age. They are tender and flavorful, making them ideal for various cooking methods.

Roaster Chicken: Larger than broilers, roaster chickens are typically between 8 to 12 weeks old and have a more developed flavor and texture. They are often roasted whole and served as a centerpiece dish.

Free-Range Chicken: These chickens are allowed to roam freely outdoors, resulting in a more natural diet and lifestyle. Many consumers prefer free-range chicken for its perceived health benefits and humane treatment.

Organic Chicken: Raised without the use of antibiotics or growth hormones, organic chickens are fed organic feed and are often raised in more humane conditions. They are popular among health-conscious consumers.

Heritage Chicken: These breeds are bred for their traditional traits and are often slower-growing than commercial broilers. They are known for their rich flavor and are favored by chefs who prioritize quality.

Beef

Grass-Fed Beef: Beef from cattle that are fed a natural diet of grass and forage. This type of beef is often leaner and has a more pronounced flavor than grain-fed beef.

Organic Beef: Raised without the use of antibiotics or growth hormones, organic beef comes from cattle that are fed organic feed and raised in humane conditions.

Filet Mignon: A premium cut of beef that is tender and flavorful, often served as a steak. It is ideal for special occasions and fine dining.

Ground Beef: Versatile and commonly used in dishes like burgers, meatballs, and tacos, ground beef can be made from various cuts and can vary in fat content.

Sushi

California Roll: A popular sushi roll that contains crab (or imitation crab), avocado, and cucumber, rolled in sushi rice and nori.

Salmon Nigiri: Slices of fresh salmon served on top of a mound of vinegared rice. This simple dish showcases the quality of the fish.

Tempura Roll: A sushi roll filled with tempura-fried vegetables or shrimp, offering a delightful crunch and flavor contrast.

Sashimi: Thinly sliced raw fish served without rice, sashimi is often served with soy sauce and wasabi, highlighting the freshness of the seafood.
